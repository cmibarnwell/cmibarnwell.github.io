Impulse Meter

(Fall 2016)

Collaborative project that involved planning using Mechanical Physics and Mathematics to find the simplest way to measure impulse (Change in momentum). Since we measured our spring constant before construction, this is performed by using a slow motion camera and observing the distance of the compression of the spring when hit by an object. Next the time until max compression must also be observed. This allows for measuring of impulse with minimal knowledge of variables, including the neglection of mass. We checked our results using conservation of energy and kinematics to compare the theoretical change in momentum.

Equations used: F = kx, J = FΔt
